Why a generic CRM (or a generic build) falls short here

Travel doesn't sell in a straight pipeline.

A generic "Lead → Deal → Won" CRM assumes one product, one price, one invoice. A travel enquiry is none of those.

🗺️

Itinerary-based selling

One enquiry can turn into 3–4 competing itinerary options before a client picks one — a "deal" needs to hold multiple quoted versions, not just one price.

🧮

Cost vs. sell price

Every package has a vendor cost (hotel, transport, guide) hidden behind the client-facing price — margin visibility per package, not just total invoice value.

👥

Group vs. solo travel

A single booking can be one traveler or a group of 20, each needing their own documents but one consolidated invoice and payment schedule.

📅

Payment tied to travel date

Advance on booking, balance before departure — reminders need to run off the travel date, not the invoice date like a typical CRM assumes.

🧾

Tour-operator GST

Package/tour invoicing has its own valuation and documentation treatment, different from a straightforward service invoice — a generic invoice template gets this wrong.

📲

WhatsApp-first enquiries

Most travel enquiries start as a WhatsApp message, not a form fill — the intake needs to meet clients there, the way this CRM already does for support tickets.

AI, in the same disciplined way

  • Score enquiries by likely trip value and booking urgency — same pattern as lead scoring today
  • Draft an itinerary reply from an enquiry's stated dates/budget/interests — human edits and sends
  • Summarize a long back-and-forth with a client before a follow-up call

Operational features a travel business specifically needs

  • Vendor/supplier ledger — track what's owed to hotels, transporters, guides per trip
  • Seasonal demand view — which months/destinations are trending in enquiries
  • Group booking roll-up — one invoice, many traveler records and documents

What's proven vs. what's new work

Not a rebuild from zero — but not a copy-paste either.

Here's exactly what carries over from the system we already run our agency on, and what would be built fresh for travel.

Reused as-is (already battle-tested)

  • GST invoicing engine — CGST/SGST/IGST auto-split, sequential legal numbering, GSTIN validation
  • Partial/milestone payment tracking against an invoice
  • Client portal architecture, role-based access, 2FA, audit logging, automated backups
  • AI discipline: opt-in, wrapped in try/catch, never auto-sends
  • Runs on the same low-cost shared hosting — no infra overhead

Built new for this vertical

  • Itinerary/package data model (multiple options per enquiry, line-item vendor cost)
  • Tour-operator-specific GST valuation and invoice format
  • Traveler/group records distinct from the paying client
  • Payment-schedule logic anchored to travel date, not invoice date
  • Vendor/supplier ledger and seasonal demand reporting
