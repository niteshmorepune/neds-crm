# Sales — Recording Script

**Audience:** sales team (watch `getting-started.md` first).
**Target length:** ~9 minutes

**Before you record:** have one existing lead, one existing client with an
open deal, and one existing quotation ready to click through. Log in as a
sales user.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro + dashboard (0:00–0:40)
**ON SCREEN:** Sales dashboard.
**SAY:** "This one's for sales. Your workflow runs lead, to deal, to
quotation, to invoice — let's walk through each step. Your dashboard shows
follow-ups due today, what you've won this month, your open pipeline by
stage, and that same overdue-tasks banner if you have one."

## Scene 2 — Adding a lead (0:40–2:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Lead Generation → Add Lead → fill the form → Save.
**SAY:** "To add a lead: Lead Generation, Add Lead. Fill in name, company,
phone or email, where they came from, which service they're interested in,
and an estimated value — assign yourself as owner. Save it, and within a
minute a score badge appears — 0 to 100, higher means more promising. Hover
over it to see why. If you edit the lead later, it re-scores automatically.
One thing to know: leads that existed before AI was turned on won't show a
badge until you open them and save an edit."

## Scene 3 — Working a lead (2:00–3:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Open the lead, add a note, then Log a call, then expand
"Add follow-up reminder."
**SAY:** "Once you're working a lead, add notes as you go — everything
shows up on the timeline. After you call them, log the call and record the
outcome. If you need a follow-up, click 'Add follow-up reminder' — the date
field starts blank on purpose, so set it to the actual day you want to be
reminded, not just tomorrow by default. Add a one-line next action. You'll
get a bell notification when that time hits, and it'll show in your
morning digest. There's also a Draft follow-up button — that's Claude
writing a suggested message for you to edit and send yourself. It never
sends anything on its own."

## Scene 4 — Converting a lead (3:30–4:15)
**ON SCREEN:** Click Convert on a lead.
**SAY:** "When it's real business, click Convert. That creates a Client and
a Deal automatically, linked together. The new client shows up as
'Prospect' in yellow — it flips to Active in green automatically the
moment you mark the deal Won."

## Scene 5 — Send Quotation shortcut (4:15–4:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a lead, click Send Quotation.
**SAY:** "You don't actually have to convert first — open any lead and
click Send Quotation. If they're not converted yet, the CRM does that for
you automatically and drops you straight into the quotation builder,
already filled in."

## Scene 6 — Pipeline (4:45–5:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Sales Department board, drag a deal between columns.
**SAY:** "Your pipeline board — New, Contacted, Proposal, Negotiation, Won
or Lost. Drag a deal to the next stage as it progresses, or open it and
change the stage directly. Won and Lost are final. If a deal came through a
partner agency rather than directly, set the 'Referred by' dropdown on the
deal — leave it as Direct otherwise."

## Scene 7 — Building a quotation (5:30–7:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a deal → + New Quotation → add a line item → add a
milestone → save → download PDF.
**SAY:** "For quotations — easiest way is opening a deal and clicking '+
New Quotation' right there, client and deal already filled in. Add your
line items — description, HSN code, quantity, rate — GST calculates
itself, CGST plus SGST for Maharashtra clients, IGST for everyone else. For
project work, add milestones — advance on signing, balance on delivery,
that kind of thing. Save, download the PDF, send it to the client. Once
they agree, mark it Accepted, then Convert to invoice — accounts takes it
from there."

## Scene 8 — Clients (7:00–8:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Clients list, then Clients → Import.
**SAY:** "Your Clients list only shows companies assigned to you plus
unassigned ones — not your teammates' clients. Add one at a time with
Clients, Add, or bulk-import a CSV with Clients, Import — download the
template first, it's got columns for address, owner, and tags. Leave Owner
blank and it assigns to you automatically. For clients outside India, set
the Country field — that hides GSTIN and State and produces a zero-rated,
no-GST invoice automatically."

## Scene 9 — Bell notifications + wrap-up (8:00–9:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Point at the bell icon.
**SAY:** "You'll get bell notifications for a new lead assigned to you, a
new quotation on your deal, your deal being marked Won, and a new invoice
for your client. And two daily emails: a 9 AM morning digest, and a 10 AM
stagnation alert if a lead's gone 7 days or a deal 10 days without any
activity — even a quick note resets that clock. Keep your notes and call
outcomes up to date; they drive your follow-up list and your performance
report."
