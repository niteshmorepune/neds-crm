# Sales — Recording Script

**Audience:** sales team (watch `getting-started.md` first).
**Target length:** ~13 minutes

**Before you record:** have one existing lead, one existing client with an
open deal (ideally with a "Deals like this one" match — another closed deal
on the same service), and one existing quotation linked to a deal with notes
ready to click through. Log in as a sales user.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro + dashboard (0:00–0:40)
**ON SCREEN:** Sales dashboard.
**SAY:** "This one's for sales. Your workflow runs lead, to deal, to
quotation, to invoice — let's walk through each step. Your dashboard shows
follow-ups due today, what you've won this month, your open pipeline by
stage, and that same overdue-tasks banner if you have one."

## Scene 2 — Adding a lead (0:40–2:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Lead Generation → Add Lead → fill the form → Save.
**SAY:** "To add a lead: Lead Generation, Add Lead. Fill in name, company,
phone or email, where they came from, which service they're interested in,
and an estimated value — assign yourself as owner. Save it, and within a
minute a score badge appears — 0 to 100, higher means more promising. Hover
over it to see why. If you edit the lead later, it re-scores automatically.
One thing to know: leads that existed before AI was turned on won't show a
badge until you open them and save an edit."

## Scene 3 — Working a lead (2:00–3:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Open the lead, add a note, click Dictate on the Notes field
and speak a line, then Log a call, then expand "Add follow-up reminder."
**SAY:** "Once you're working a lead, add notes as you go — everything
shows up on the timeline. On the Notes field, you can click Dictate and
just speak instead of typing — your browser transcribes it live, and you
can still edit before saving. That's Chrome or Edge only; the button
simply won't show up elsewhere. After you call them, log the call and
record the outcome. If you need a follow-up, click 'Add follow-up
reminder' — the date field starts blank on purpose, so set it to the
actual day you want to be reminded, not just tomorrow by default. Add a
one-line next action. You'll get a bell notification when that time hits,
and it'll show in your morning digest. There's also a Draft follow-up
button — that's Claude writing a suggested message for you to edit and
send yourself. It never sends anything on its own."

## Scene 4 — Converting a lead (3:45–4:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Click Convert on a lead.
**SAY:** "When it's real business, click Convert. That creates a Client and
a Deal automatically, linked together. The new client shows up as
'Prospect' in yellow — it flips to Active in green automatically the
moment you mark the deal Won."

## Scene 5 — Send Quotation shortcut (4:30–5:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a lead, click Send Quotation.
**SAY:** "You don't actually have to convert first — open any lead and
click Send Quotation. If they're not converted yet, the CRM does that for
you automatically and drops you straight into the quotation builder,
already filled in."

## Scene 6 — Pipeline board (5:00–6:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Sales Department board, drag a deal between columns, point
at a stale-deal badge.
**SAY:** "Your pipeline board — New, Contacted, Proposal, Negotiation, Won
or Lost. Drag a deal to the next stage as it progresses, or open it and
change the stage directly. Won and Lost are final on the stage — though the
Value stays correctable afterwards if the final number changes, and every
report picks that up automatically. Each card shows how many days it's
been sitting in its current stage, turning red past 10 — that's a nudge to
follow up, not a penalty. If a deal came through a partner agency rather
than directly, set the 'Referred by' dropdown on the deal — leave it as
Direct otherwise."

## Scene 7 — Pipeline KPIs and the Sales Dashboard (6:00–7:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Point at the KPI strip above the board (open pipeline,
weighted forecast, won this month/FY, win rate, avg deal size, avg sales
cycle), then Sidebar → Sales Dashboard.
**SAY:** "Above the board, this KPI strip is your own numbers at a glance —
open pipeline, a weighted forecast that adjusts for how likely each stage
actually is to close, won this month and this financial year, win rate,
average deal size, average sales cycle. For the fuller picture, there's a
whole Sales Dashboard page in the sidebar — same numbers, plus a 12-month
won-value trend chart, a service-line breakdown so you can see what's
actually converting, a Needs Attention list of deals that are stale,
overdue on follow-up, or sitting at a ₹0 value, and — if your manager's set
one — a target-vs-actual progress bar."

## Scene 8 — Deals like this one (7:30–8:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a deal with at least one comparable closed deal, scroll
to the "Deals like this one" panel.
**SAY:** "One more thing on a deal's page — 'Deals like this one' shows up
to three other closed deals for the same service, ranked by how close their
value is to this one. Useful context on how deals with this profile
actually tend to go, both wins and losses. It needs a service set on the
deal, and at least one other closed deal in that service to compare
against."

## Scene 9 — Building a quotation (8:00–9:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a deal → + New Quotation → click ✨ Suggest line items
→ review the suggested lines → add a milestone → save → download PDF.
**SAY:** "For quotations — easiest way is opening a deal and clicking '+
New Quotation' right there, client and deal already filled in. If the deal
has notes on it, try '✨ Suggest line items' first — Claude drafts a first
pass at the description, quantity, and SAC code for each line based only
on what's actually in those notes. It never fills in a rate or a GST
percent — those stay blank on every suggested line, exactly like a
manually-added one, so you're still the one pricing and saving it. If the
deal has no notes, it'll just say so instead of guessing. GST calculates
itself once you fill in a rate — CGST plus SGST for Maharashtra clients,
IGST for everyone else. For project work, add milestones — advance on
signing, balance on delivery, that kind of thing. Save, download the PDF,
send it to the client — or click Send to Client to email it straight from
here. Once they agree, mark it Accepted, then Convert to invoice —
accounts takes it from there."

## Scene 10 — Clients (9:45–10:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Clients list, then Clients → Import, then point at the
Non-GST client checkbox on a client's edit page.
**SAY:** "Your Clients list only shows companies assigned to you plus
unassigned ones — not your teammates' clients. Add one at a time with
Clients, Add, or bulk-import a CSV with Clients, Import — download the
template first, it's got columns for address, owner, and tags. Leave Owner
blank and it assigns to you automatically. For clients outside India, set
the Country field — that hides GSTIN and State and produces a zero-rated,
no-GST invoice automatically. For a domestic client who's arranged to be
billed with no GST, tick 'Non-GST client' instead — different from
Country, they're still in India, we're just not charging GST on their
bills."

## Scene 11 — AI content drafts on a client (10:45–11:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a client → Notes tab → point at an "AI-drafted monthly
update" note. Then open one of your projects and point at the "Pending
Client Update" panel.
**SAY:** "Two AI drafts worth knowing about that show up on their own. On
the 1st of each month, if a client had any activity the month before —
tasks done, tickets resolved, a payment, project posts — Claude drafts a
short 'here's what we delivered' note on their Notes tab. It's staff-only;
review it, personalize it, and send it yourself if it reads right. And on
any project you run, if a task was completed that day, you'll see a
Pending Client Update panel most evenings — edit it if needed, then click
Approve & Send to post it straight to the client's portal and email their
billing contact in one step, or Discard if it's not worth sharing. Nothing
reaches the client until you click Approve & Send."

## Scene 12 — Bell notifications + wrap-up (11:45–13:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Point at the bell icon.
**SAY:** "You'll get bell notifications for a new lead assigned to you, a
nurture follow-up Claude drafted when a lead's gone quiet, a new quotation
on your deal, your deal being marked Won, a new invoice for your client,
your monthly wins note being drafted, and a project update ready for your
review. And two daily emails: a 9 AM morning digest, and a 10 AM
stagnation alert if a lead's gone 7 days or a deal 10 days without any
activity — even a quick note resets that clock. Keep your notes and call
outcomes up to date; they drive your follow-up list and your performance
report."
