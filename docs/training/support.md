# Support — Recording Script

**Audience:** support team (watch `getting-started.md` first).
**Target length:** ~8 minutes

**Before you record:** have one open ticket and one project you're assigned
to ready to click through. Log in as a support user.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro + dashboard (0:00–0:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Support dashboard.
**SAY:** "This one's for support. You handle two things day to day: tickets
— client issues — and project updates for delivery work. Your dashboard
shows open tickets, how many are at risk of breaching SLA, and a breakdown
by priority."

## Scene 2 — Opening and replying to a ticket (0:30–2:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a ticket, type a reply, tick Internal note on a second
example, click Draft with AI if enabled.
**SAY:** "Tickets come in from the client portal or get raised by staff.
Open one to see the conversation, priority, and SLA due time — urgent ones
are due soonest. Type in the reply box and send — that emails the client
directly. If you want to leave a note only staff can see, tick 'Internal
note' first — that one doesn't get emailed. If AI is turned on, 'Draft with
AI' writes you a suggested reply from the conversation — read it, edit it,
then send. 'Summarize thread' gives you a quick recap on a long ticket."

## Scene 3 — Managing and filtering tickets (2:00–3:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Change status/priority/assignee on the ticket, then go to
the tickets list and tick the SLA breached filter.
**SAY:** "From the panel you can change status, priority, or reassign,
attach files, and mark it resolved once you're done — the client's
notified automatically. On the tickets list, use the filter bar to narrow
by status or priority, and tick 'SLA breached' to instantly see just the
ones that have already blown past their deadline — good way to start your
day."

## Scene 4 — WhatsApp tickets and Drishti link (3:00–3:40)
**ON SCREEN:** Point at a WhatsApp-badged ticket, and a Drishti context bar
if one's available.
**SAY:** "If a client messages your WhatsApp number, a ticket gets created
automatically — you'll see this green WhatsApp badge. Handle it like any
other ticket. And if the client's connected to Drishti, you'll see a blue
'Open in Drishti' bar right above the description — click it to jump
straight to the relevant view instead of switching tabs manually."

## Scene 5 — Project updates and client-visible notes (3:40–5:15)
**ON SCREEN:** Open Project Updates → a project → scroll to Notes & Client
Updates → type a note, show the "Visible to client" checkbox ticked.
**SAY:** "Project Updates shows the projects you're on. Scroll down to
Notes & Client Updates to post something — 'Visible to client' is ticked
by default, meaning it shows up in the client's portal. Leave it ticked for
normal updates; untick it only for internal hand-off notes the client
shouldn't see. You can edit or delete your own notes anytime — managers can
edit or delete anyone's. One thing to flag: support can't add or edit
client contacts — that's sales' job — but you can view them fine."

## Scene 6 — Tasks and scheduled maintenance (5:15–6:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Employee Task list, point at a task with an obvious
"maintenance" style title.
**SAY:** "Employee Task is your task list — move things from To-do through
In Progress, Review, to Done as you work. Every morning at 8 AM the CRM
auto-creates maintenance tasks for each active project you lead — things
like backups, security scans, plugin updates, ranking reports — based on
what service that project runs. You'll get a bell notification, they're
already linked to the right project, just open, do the work, mark Done.
Don't let these pile up overdue — they count against your on-time
percentage in the performance report."

## Scene 7 — Notifications and wrap-up (6:30–8:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Bell icon, then mention the 9 AM email.
**SAY:** "The bell fires when a task's assigned to you or a call follow-up
comes due — that's how the CRM reaches you without email. And at 9 AM
Monday through Saturday you'll get a digest with your overdue tasks, tasks
due today, and open tickets, so you know exactly where to start before you
even open the CRM. Two habits that matter most: always set the right
priority on a ticket — it sets the SLA clock — and keep your task statuses
current."
