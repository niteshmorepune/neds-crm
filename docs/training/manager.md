# Manager — Recording Script

**Audience:** managers (watch `getting-started.md` first — includes 2FA
setup, which is required for this role).
**Target length:** ~15 minutes

**Before you record:** have a client with multiple projects, a client
flagged on Client Radar, an open ticket past SLA, a pending leave request,
and (if set up) one content piece and one partner ready to point at. Log in
as a manager user.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro + company dashboard (0:00–1:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Manager dashboard — stat cards, services donut, task
summary.
**SAY:** "This one's for managers. Unlike the operational roles, you see
the whole business at a glance — total, active, and inactive clients with
month-over-month change, a donut of projects by service line, and a task
summary: assigned, pending, overdue, completed. From here you've also got
quick links to Daily Reports and the Project Dashboard."

## Scene 2 — Client Radar (1:00–2:15)
**ON SCREEN:** Sidebar → Client Radar, point at flag types, click ✨
Suggest action on a flagged client, then ✨ Draft recovery message on a Low
Satisfaction flag.
**SAY:** "Client Radar is worth checking regularly. It flags active
clients worth a proactive check-in — No Contact, if nobody's touched them
in 14 days; Declining Activity; an Overdue Invoice; a Growth Opportunity,
where they're only using one service even though more are active; and Low
Satisfaction, if a client rated a resolved ticket 2 out of 5 or worse.
Everything here is computed live, nothing's stored. Click 'Suggest action'
on any flagged client and Claude drafts a specific next step from that
client's actual flags. On a Low Satisfaction flag specifically, there's a
second button — Draft recovery message — that writes an apology grounded
in the actual ticket that was rated poorly. Review and personalize before
sending either one; nothing goes out on its own."

## Scene 3 — Employee Performance and Revenue reports (2:15–3:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Dashboard → Reports → Employee Performance Report, pick a
month, click ✨ Generate AI Summary, then Revenue Report.
**SAY:** "Two reports you'll use a lot. Employee Performance, per person for
a chosen month — tasks completed, on-time percentage, calls made, leads
converted, attendance percentage, daily reports submitted. Click Generate
AI Summary and Claude turns those numbers into a narrative of trends and
standouts — for a Sales rep with enough pipeline history, it'll name a
specific stage where their deals move slower than the team average, not
just a vague 'may need support' line. This is Admin/Manager only — it's
never shown to the employee it's about, so treat it as a conversation
starter, not something you hand them. Export the table to CSV for
appraisals. Revenue Report, for a financial year — income by month, by
service, by client, split recurring versus one-time."

## Scene 4 — Lead sources, AI usage, and Ask the CRM (3:45–5:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Reports → Lead Source Performance, then AI Usage Report,
then Ask the CRM — type a question and show the answer.
**SAY:** "Lead Source Performance shows conversion rate by source — website,
WhatsApp, referral, cold call, phone enquiry, and so on — plus, for
tracked website leads, a breakdown by ad campaign, so you know what's
actually worth the spend. AI Usage Report tells you which of the CRM's AI
features are actually getting used — calls, tokens, an estimated cost per
feature, plus a Feedback column from anyone who's rated a draft helpful or
not, so you can tell a feature that's used a lot but trusted poorly from
one nobody's touched. And Ask the CRM is a plain-English question box —
'which clients are at risk this month', 'what's our win rate on SEO
deals' — grounded entirely in the real figures from these reports, never
invented. If it doesn't recognise your question, it lists what it can
currently answer instead of guessing, and every answer links back to the
full report and shows the exact numbers it used."

## Scene 5 — Sales Dashboard, company-wide (5:30–6:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Sidebar → Sales Dashboard → point at the rep leaderboard,
then Save Targets form and a "Suggested: ₹…" hint.
**SAY:** "Sales Dashboard, company-wide for you — same KPI strip, trend
chart, and needs-attention list as sales sees for their own deals, but
unscoped. Two sections are yours specifically: a rep leaderboard —
pipeline, won this month, target percent, win rate, average deal size, per
Sales rep — and a Save Targets form where you set the company's monthly
and financial-year target, plus each rep's own monthly target. Leave a
field blank to leave it unchanged, it'll never zero one out on you. If
there's enough recent history, you'll see a 'Suggested: ₹…' hint under a
blank field — that's the trailing three months' average won value plus 10
percent — click it to fill the field, or just type your own number."

## Scene 6 — Collections and Cash Forecast (6:45–8:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Sidebar → Collections, then Reports → Cash Forecast.
**SAY:** "Collections is the same client-by-client follow-up worklist
accounts uses — who hasn't paid, what's partial, broken payment promises,
and for milestone projects, whether the next milestone's ready to invoice.
Filter by all clients, direct only, or one partner. Cash Forecast blends
recurring revenue expected and receivables already due, bucketed by the
next three months, with the open pipeline's weighted forecast shown
separately since it's indicative, not committed cash."

## Scene 7 — Bell notifications and digests (8:00–9:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Bell icon.
**SAY:** "As a manager you're notified for a fair amount: any deal marked
Won, company-wide; the same 7-day recurring-invoice-due warning accounts
gets; a payment promise broken; a contract renewal coming due within 30
days; an SMDost brief approved and needing pricing; and any leave request
submitted or reviewed. On top of the usual 9 AM morning digest, you also
get a 9:15 AM Project Updates Digest — how many client updates were
drafted and sent yesterday, which are still awaiting review after two-plus
days, and any project that's gone quiet for five-plus days — and, every
Monday at 9 AM, a weekly owner digest synthesizing pipeline, MRR, cash
expected, and how many clients Client Radar has flagged, also as a
Monday dashboard banner."

## Scene 8 — Oversight: who's working what (9:00–10:15)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a client → Services tab → point at the Team column.
Then Project Updates → toggle My Services.
**SAY:** "You have access to every module for oversight — leads, deals,
invoices, projects, tickets, all of it, and the search bar gets you
anywhere fast. The fastest way to answer 'who handles GMB for this client'
is opening their profile, Services tab — the Team column shows the lead and
any other team members per project. And on Project Updates, there's a My
Services toggle if you also run your own accounts, so you're not scrolling
past everyone else's."

## Scene 9 — Attendance, leave approvals, and daily reports (10:15–11:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Attendance → switch team member in the dropdown →
Corrections → pick a date → update status/notes. Then Leave Requests →
Review pending → approve one, pointing at its Type. Then Daily Reports →
Team → point at a submission-streak badge.
**SAY:** "For attendance, switch between team members with the dropdown to
see anyone's monthly record. If someone forgot to check in, go to
Corrections, pick the date, and update their status and notes — the actual
check-in and check-out times come from the employee's own action or the
biometric machine and can't be edited here directly; corrections are logged
to the audit trail either way. Leave Requests has a Review pending queue —
you can't approve your own request, but any other admin or manager can.
Watch the Type column: approving a Full Day request marks attendance as
Leave for each office day in the range, but a Half Day request only marks
that one day as Half Day, not a full day off. And on Daily Reports, the
Team view shows everyone's submission for the day, with an X-out-of-7
badge per person so a chronic non-submitter stands out — red, amber, or
green."

## Scene 10 — Scheduled maintenance tasks (11:45–12:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a project with auto-created tasks.
**SAY:** "Every morning at 8 AM the CRM auto-creates maintenance tasks for
each active project — backups, security scans, that kind of thing —
assigned to the project lead. What you should watch: whether these are
piling up overdue, since they count in the performance report, and that
projects are marked Active with a service set, or they won't get any
tasks at all."

## Scene 11 — Content collaboration and partner health (12:30–13:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Sidebar → Partners → click a partner name → point at their
collections/delivery table → then a project's Content Pieces card.
**SAY:** "If we're working with an outside content agency, that's the
Content Collaboration module. Add the agency once under Partners, then on
a project you can add content pieces — agency-led, where they create
everything, or NEDS-led, where we write the copy first. Move each piece
through its stages with the Move to button, and if you need the partner to
send files back, generate a secure upload link — they don't need a CRM
login at all. Click into a partner's own page and you'll see the same
collections table filtered to just their clients — handy before a check-in
call with them, so you can answer 'how are my clients doing' without
digging. This is covered in a lot more depth in the written manager guide
if you need the full workflow."

## Scene 12 — Automated integrations, brief overview (13:45–14:30)
**ON SCREEN:** A client's Activity tab showing an automated event.
**SAY:** "Quick note on the automated side — when a deal is marked Won, the
client gets set up in Drishti and SMDost automatically, no manual entry.
Every one of these automated events leaves a trace on the client's Activity
tab, so if something seems off, that's the first place to check. Full
troubleshooting steps are in the Integrations guide."

## Scene 13 — Wrap-up (14:30–15:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Dashboard, point at SLA-at-risk widget.
**SAY:** "Two habits worth building: check Client Radar and SLA-at-risk
tickets regularly, and let the stagnation alert emails do the chasing for
cold leads and deals — you don't need to track that manually. That covers
the manager essentials."
