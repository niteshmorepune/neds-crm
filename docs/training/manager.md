# Manager — Recording Script

**Audience:** managers (watch `getting-started.md` first — includes 2FA
setup, which is required for this role).
**Target length:** ~9 minutes

**Before you record:** have a client with multiple projects, an open ticket
past SLA, and (if set up) one content piece and one partner ready to point
at. Log in as a manager user.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro + company dashboard (0:00–1:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Manager dashboard — stat cards, services donut, task
summary.
**SAY:** "This one's for managers. Unlike the operational roles, you see
the whole business at a glance — total, active, and inactive clients with
month-over-month change, a donut of projects by service line, and a task
summary: assigned, pending, overdue, completed. From here you've also got
quick links to Daily Reports and the Project Dashboard."

## Scene 2 — Reports (1:00–2:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Dashboard → Reports → Employee Performance Report, pick a
month, then Revenue Report.
**SAY:** "Two reports you'll use a lot. Employee Performance, per person for
a chosen month — tasks completed, on-time percentage, calls made, leads
converted, attendance percentage, daily reports submitted. Export to CSV
for appraisals. Revenue Report, for a financial year — income by month, by
service, by client, split recurring versus one-time. There's also
Outstanding Receivables, shared with accounts, showing what clients still
owe."

## Scene 3 — Bell notifications (2:30–3:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Bell icon.
**SAY:** "As a manager you get notified when any deal is marked Won,
company-wide — not just your own — plus the same 7-day recurring invoice
warning accounts gets, and when an SMDost brief gets approved and needs
pricing."

## Scene 4 — Oversight: who's working what (3:00–4:15)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a client → Services tab → point at the Team column.
Then Project Updates → toggle My Services.
**SAY:** "You have access to every module for oversight — leads, deals,
invoices, projects, tickets, all of it, and the search bar gets you
anywhere fast. The fastest way to answer 'who handles GMB for this client'
is opening their profile, Services tab — the Team column shows the lead and
any other team members per project. And on Project Updates, there's a My
Services toggle if you also run your own accounts, so you're not scrolling
past everyone else's."

## Scene 5 — Attendance corrections (4:15–5:15)
**ON SCREEN:** Attendance → switch team member in the dropdown → 
Corrections → pick a date → update status/notes.
**SAY:** "For attendance, switch between team members with the dropdown to
see anyone's monthly record. If someone forgot to check in, go to
Corrections, pick the date, and update their status and notes — the actual
check-in and check-out times come from the employee's own action or the
biometric machine and can't be edited here directly; corrections are logged
to the audit trail either way."

## Scene 6 — Scheduled maintenance tasks (5:15–6:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Open a project with auto-created tasks.
**SAY:** "Every morning at 8 AM the CRM auto-creates maintenance tasks for
each active project — backups, security scans, that kind of thing —
assigned to the project lead. What you should watch: whether these are
piling up overdue, since they count in the performance report, and that
projects are marked Active with a service set, or they won't get any
tasks at all."

## Scene 7 — Content collaboration, brief overview (6:00–7:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Sidebar → Partners → then a project's Content Pieces card.
**SAY:** "If we're working with an outside content agency, that's the
Content Collaboration module. Add the agency once under Partners, then on
a project you can add content pieces — agency-led, where they create
everything, or NEDS-led, where we write the copy first. Move each piece
through its stages with the Move to button, and if you need the partner to
send files back, generate a secure upload link — they don't need a CRM
login at all. This is covered in a lot more depth in the written manager
guide if you need the full workflow."

## Scene 8 — Automated integrations, brief overview (7:30–8:15)
**ON SCREEN:** A client's Activity tab showing an automated event.
**SAY:** "Quick note on the automated side — when a deal is marked Won, the
client gets set up in Drishti and SMDost automatically, no manual entry.
Every one of these automated events leaves a trace on the client's Activity
tab, so if something seems off, that's the first place to check. Full
troubleshooting steps are in the Integrations guide."

## Scene 9 — Wrap-up (8:15–9:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Dashboard, point at SLA-at-risk widget.
**SAY:** "Two habits worth building: check SLA-at-risk tickets and the
overdue follow-ups widget regularly, and let the stagnation alert emails do
the chasing for cold leads and deals — you don't need to track that
manually. That covers the manager essentials."
