# Accounts — Recording Script

**Audience:** accounts team (watch `getting-started.md` first).
**Target length:** ~8 minutes

**Before you record:** have one invoice with a "Pending Invoice #" badge,
one already-numbered invoice, and one recurring invoice template ready.
Log in as an accounts user.

---

## Scene 1 — Intro + dashboard (0:00–0:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Accounts dashboard.
**SAY:** "This one's for accounts. You own invoices, payments, recurring
billing, and the revenue reports. Your dashboard shows outstanding
receivables, what's been collected this month, and how many invoices are
overdue."

## Scene 2 — GST-compliant invoices (0:30–1:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Open an invoice, point at the GSTIN, tax breakup, and
amount-in-words sections.
**SAY:** "Every invoice here is GST-compliant. Maharashtra clients get
CGST plus SGST; other Indian states get IGST at the full rate; overseas
clients are zero-rated automatically — no GST, and the PDF says 'Export of
Services' instead — that's driven entirely by the Country field on the
client, nothing to set manually. Each invoice number looks like
NEDS/2026-27/0042, shows both parties' GSTINs, HSN codes, the tax breakup,
and the amount in words."

## Scene 3 — Assigning the invoice number (1:30–2:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Open the invoice with the pending badge, click Assign
Invoice Number.
**SAY:** "When sales converts a quotation to an invoice, it lands here with
a yellow 'Pending Invoice #' badge — no GST number yet. Open it and click
Assign Invoice Number to generate the next sequential number. You can't
send or download the PDF until you do this — it's the gate that keeps
numbering sequential and correct."

## Scene 4 — Recording a payment (2:30–4:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Open an invoice → Record a payment → fill amount/date/mode
→ tick the receipt checkbox → Record.
**SAY:** "To record a payment, open the invoice, fill in amount, date, and
mode — UPI, NEFT, cheque, cash, or gateway. Partial payments are fine — the
invoice moves to Partially Paid, then Paid once it's fully settled,
automatically. If the client has an email on file, you'll see a 'Send
payment receipt to client' checkbox — tick it before you click Record and
they get an emailed confirmation with the amount, mode, and remaining
balance. Once you save, a bell notification goes out to the rest of
accounts and the client's sales rep — purely informational, nothing for
you to do there."

## Scene 5 — Recurring invoices (4:00–6:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Invoices → Recurring Invoices → open a template → show its
generated-invoice history → Send Email on one row.
**SAY:** "For monthly retainers — SEO, GMB, social, ads, AMC — set up a
recurring template here. Every morning the scheduler checks for due
templates and auto-generates and emails the invoice — you don't have to do
anything. Click into a template to see its full history of generated
invoices; from there you can resend an invoice by email, or click View/Pay
to record a payment on any of them, same as a normal invoice. Clients also
get automatic reminder emails 7, 5, 3, and 1 day before each billing date —
again, no action from you. And every morning at 8 AM, if any recurring
invoice is due in exactly 7 days and unpaid, you and admin/managers get a
bell notification — gives you a full week's notice."

## Scene 6 — Deleting a recurring template (6:00–6:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Point at the Delete button on a template.
**SAY:** "If a retainer ends, open the template and delete it — that
removes the template and its future schedule, but every invoice it already
generated stays untouched for your records."

## Scene 7 — Reports and wrap-up (6:30–8:00)
**ON SCREEN:** Dashboard → Reports → Revenue Report, then the sidebar
Account → outstanding receivables report.
**SAY:** "For reporting: the sidebar's Account report shows outstanding
receivables — who owes what and how overdue. From the dashboard's Reports
panel, the Revenue Report breaks income down by month, by service, and by
client, split recurring versus one-time — export either to CSV. Two habits
that matter: assign the invoice number before trying to send anything — the
button's disabled until you do — and record payments promptly so the
dashboard numbers stay accurate. Also worth knowing, none of the daily
digest or reminder emails go out on Sundays."
